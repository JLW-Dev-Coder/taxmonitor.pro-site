Featured Analysis

The OIC That Should Never Have Been Filed

A detailed examination of structural failure in Offer in Compromise processing.

The Problem

Client paid $7,500 for OIC preparation and filing. Application was rejected. Collection statute had 14 months remaining—the liability would have expired before any OIC processing completed.

What Was Assumed

Client owed $84,000. OIC was the "best option" based on income and asset evaluation. Filing would protect from enforcement.

What the Data Revealed

Collection Statute Expiration Date (CSED) was December 2024. Average OIC processing time: 18-24 months. Even if accepted, client would pay more than waiting.

Financial / Ethical Risk

$7,500 fee for inappropriate service. OIC filing suspended collection statute—adding months to liability. Client harm compounded by solution design.

Structural Lesson

CSED analysis must precede any OIC recommendation. This isn't about due diligence—it's about having account data before offering solutions.

Resolution First

  • Intake call presents OIC as solution
  • Fee collected before transcript review
  • CSED discovered after filing
  • Client pays for inappropriate service
  • Statute suspended, extending liability

Clarity First

  • Transcript analysis before consultation
  • CSED identified in initial review
  • OIC ruled out as option
  • Monitoring strategy recommended
  • Client protected from unnecessary cost
